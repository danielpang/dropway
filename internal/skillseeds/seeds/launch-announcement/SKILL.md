# Launch announcement

Use this skill when asked to announce a feature, product, or release. Gather
the brief first, then produce every deliverable in one pass so the messaging
stays consistent.

## 1. Gather the brief

Ask for (or extract from context):
- What shipped, in one sentence a customer would understand.
- Who it's for and the pain it removes.
- One concrete before/after example or metric.
- Availability: plans, pricing, rollout timing, any limits.

If any of these are missing, ask — don't invent capabilities or numbers.

## 2. Positioning line

Write one sentence in the form: *For <audience> who <pain>, <product> now
<capability> so <outcome>.* Every other deliverable derives from this line.

## 3. Deliverables

Produce all of these, in this order:

- **Announcement post** (300–500 words): lead with the customer outcome, show
  the before/after example, explain how it works in two short paragraphs, end
  with availability and a single call to action.
- **Changelog entry** (2–4 sentences): plain, factual, no superlatives. What
  changed, who gets it, where to find it.
- **Social copy**: three variants under 280 characters — one outcome-led, one
  example-led, one question-led. No hashtag stuffing; at most one link.

## Voice rules

- Concrete beats clever: "deploys in 8 seconds" beats "blazing fast".
- Say "you", not "users". Present tense. No "we're excited to announce".
- Claims must trace back to the brief; if it isn't in the brief, cut it.
